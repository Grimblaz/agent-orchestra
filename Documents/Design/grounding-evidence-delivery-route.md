# Grounding Evidence Delivery Route

`/design`'s Grounding Discipline (see `skills/design-exploration/SKILL.md` § Grounding Discipline) verifies that every artifact a design names is traceable to the live repository, and produces a `**Grounding Evidence**` block as the audit trail of that verification. This document covers the route that block takes from the design session to the durable artifact a downstream check actually reads — a route that, until issue #866, did not exist.

## Problem

The Issue-Planner standards check at `skills/upstream-onboarding/SKILL.md:303` fires on design-body pickup and requires a `**Grounding Evidence**` row for every artifact the design body names — plus, on a second clause, an accompanying escalation note for any row disposed `could-not-ground-escalate`. But nothing in the producer contract ever told Solution-Designer to put the block there.

The break was structural, not a naming slip:

- `skills/design-exploration/SKILL.md` instructed the block to be written "into the design session" — an ephemeral surface that evaporates at end of session.
- The durable-payload enumeration (the design-exploration skill's own "prepare what to persist" section) and the Solution-Designer Stage 4 persist step both listed decisions, acceptance criteria, testing scope, and rejected alternatives — and both omitted the Grounding Evidence block.
- The consumer, meanwhile, reads the durable design body, which link 1 never wrote to.

A designer following both persist contracts to the letter still fails the downstream check. Measurement across the design corpus (issue #866 body) found only 1 of 7 designs carried any Grounding Evidence content in the body at design-exit, and that one success used a non-canonical `## Grounding Evidence` H2 heading rather than the bold-heading shape the consumer names — evidence that, absent a template, the one designer who remembered to persist it still improvised a shape the check doesn't recognize. A second, later measurement of current body state (after two of the seven bodies were hand-backfilled at downstream checkpoints) found 1 of 7 in the fully canonical shape, with the other backfills landing in different non-canonical shapes — three designers, three different drift outcomes, reinforcing that the absent template rather than carelessness was the cause.

The identical discipline one phase downstream — `plan-authoring`'s Tree-State Verification, producing a `**Verification Evidence**` block — does not suffer this defect, because its sentinel and heading live inside the persisted plan-markdown template itself. The design side had no design-markdown template at all; that asymmetry was the entire defect. See issue #866 for the full corpus tables and the discovery history (first surfaced by the Issue-Planner standards check on #863, then reproduced identically one day later on #842).

## What shipped

The fix is a dual-surface persist, not a relocation:

- **Unchanged**: the in-session write and the pre-challenge absence gate in `skills/design-exploration/SKILL.md` § Grounding Discipline continue exactly as before.
- **Added**: at Solution-Designer Stage 4 (issue update), the same block — reconciled per the rule below — is **additionally persisted** into the durable design body, alongside decisions, acceptance criteria, testing scope, and rejected alternatives.

The canonical shape is a `<!-- grounding-evidence -->` sentinel line immediately above a `**Grounding Evidence** (HEAD: {sha})` bold heading and table (see `skills/design-exploration/SKILL.md` § Durable evidence block for the literal template). Bold heading, not H2, because the consumer literal at `upstream-onboarding/SKILL.md:303` names the bold text and the corpus's own H2 improvisation is the documented drift case.

Beyond the placement fix itself, the design and implementation add:

- **Escalation-note rule**: whenever any row carries `could-not-ground-escalate`, one or more `**Escalation note — {artifact}**: {reason}` prose lines must persist directly under the table as one unit — satisfying the consumer's second clause. Escalation notes are exempt from compaction.
- **Body-size compact gate**: the design body has a hard 65,536-codepoint cap. When persisting the full table would push projected body size past roughly 55,000 codepoints, a compact form persists instead — one row per artifact with disposition and a one-line inference digest, quadrant citations collapsed to the single most load-bearing `path:line` — with a `compact mode: full table was N KB` note. The compact form still opens with the same sentinel and bold heading; only the row detail shrinks. An overflow floor halts and surfaces rather than silently attempting a write that still won't fit.
- **Persist-time re-grounding/re-stamping**: before persisting, any artifact touched by an Incorporate or `grounded-conflict` revision during the design challenge is re-grounded, and the HEAD stamp is refreshed only when re-grounding actually occurred; otherwise the original write-time stamp stands.
- **Placement anchor**: the block lands immediately after the `<!-- named-decisions:end -->` sentinel when present (resolved to the last occurrence outside a code span), else as the final block of the body — deterministic on both rich and greenfield bodies.
- **Completion Gate checklist item**: `agents/Solution-Designer.agent.md` gains an explicit checkbox for "Grounding Evidence block (with escalation notes when applicable) persisted in the issue body," giving the placement rule an in-band forcing function rather than relying on prose alone.
- **A new corpus-check diagnostic**: `.github/scripts/grounding-evidence-corpus-check.ps1` classifies a design-issue body as `canonical | non-canonical | absent` using structural, span-aware detection (the sentinel immediately followed by the bold heading, both outside fenced and inline code spans) rather than substring counting — so a body that merely *discusses* the sentinel and heading in prose is never mistaken for one that actually *contains* the block. This is explicitly **not** a tracking mechanism; nothing fires it automatically. See the script's own docstring `HONESTY DISCLOSURE` section for the full limitation statement, summarized below.

## Review history worth preserving

The value of the pipeline this fix went through is best illustrated by what it actually caught, not just the state it landed on.

**The CE Gate method changed mid-plan, and the reason is itself a finding.** The original plan approved a live-`/design`-run CE Gate behind a plugin-cache refresh and a Claude Code restart. That approach was abandoned before implementation, because the *identical* staged-manual gate shape had already been used to validate this exact discipline once before — on #763, the issue that created the Grounding Discipline in the first place — and it false-passed: PR #766 merged with no CE Gate evidence artifact, and 6 of the 7 subsequent designs went on to drop the block anyway. Re-running the same validation shape a second time would have repeated a demonstrated failure. The plan was amended to a persona-blind, mutation-tested contract-sufficiency exercise instead: a subagent is handed the Stage-4 persist task *without* the Grounding Evidence block named in the task statement, and must produce it anyway because the persist enumeration itself instructs it to — with an explicit mutation check confirming the exercise genuinely fails on a tree with the enumeration reverted. A corpus-baseline scan is recorded as a dated measurement, not a pinned assertion, because the live issue bodies it reads are mutable.

**The plan itself was stress-tested twice.** The first full round ran the standard five-pass two-layer prosecution panel against the original plan and produced several sustained findings, most notably that the live-run CE Gate depended on stale-plugin-cache handling that the amendment later removed entirely rather than patched. A second, delta-scoped round then prosecuted the amendment itself (one opus pass, adjudicated by parent verification rather than a full defense/judge cycle, and labeled as prosecution-only rather than presented as a complete pipeline) and returned 14 findings, all 14 sustained — including that the amendment's first draft named the Grounding Evidence block directly in the mutation-check task statement, which meant the exercise could never fail regardless of whether the actual fix worked, and that the corpus partition the amendment assumed as ground truth was itself wrong on two of the seven issues once the structural classifier was actually run against them.

**The code review caught two real, independently-reproduced high-severity bugs in the corpus-check script's own detection logic** — the exact kind of defect the script exists to prevent misjudging in others. CRLF line endings (the actual line-ending shape of every real GitHub API response body) silently broke fenced-code-block exclusion, because the fence-line regex's end anchor never matched a CRLF-terminated line — so a canonical block quoted inside a fenced code example would misclassify as a real canonical block rather than as absent. Separately, inline-backtick-span pairing was not bounded to a single paragraph, so two unrelated stray backtick runs sitting in separate paragraphs could pair up across the gap and swallow a real block sitting between them. Both defects defeated the script's own stated "structural, not substring-counting" guarantee on its single most likely real-world input shape. Both were fixed — normalizing line endings once at the public entry point, and bounding inline-span pairing to a single paragraph — and both were locked with new regression fixtures.

Taken together, these are a concrete argument for why the adversarial pipeline (prosecution → defense → judge, plus mutation-tested CE Gate exercises) earns its cost: a plan amendment corrected a validation method with a documented false-pass history, a delta review caught the amendment's own vacuous mutation check before it shipped, and a code review caught the diagnostic tool misjudging its own primary target case.

## Known limitations

Stated plainly, because softening them would recreate the same class of overclaim this issue exists to fix:

- **The corpus-check script is an end-state diagnostic, not a tracking mechanism.** Nothing in the pipeline invokes it automatically — it is a manual, on-demand maintainer tool. A high canonical rate returned by a future run is not proof this fix "worked"; it is equally consistent with "designers still drop the block, and the downstream Issue-Planner lens keeps catching and backfilling it," which is the exact failure mode this issue set out to close. The script's own docstring carries this disclosure verbatim and deliberately implements no backfill-detection heuristic (rejected during planning as too fragile to be trustworthy).
- **The script cannot distinguish who persisted the block.** A canonical-shaped block in a body today could have been written by Solution-Designer at Stage 4, exactly as intended, or hand-backfilled later by a human or a downstream agent reacting to the standards check — both #863 and #842 are real, documented instances of the latter, and the script has no way to tell the two apart from the body content alone.
- **The real self-firing detector for whether this fix holds is unchanged by this issue.** It remains the pre-existing Issue-Planner standards check at `skills/upstream-onboarding/SKILL.md:303` — the only link in the original chain that was already correct. That check runs on every design→plan pickup with no additional wiring; the corpus-check script is a supplementary, occasional cross-check, not a replacement for it.

## Where things live

- `skills/design-exploration/SKILL.md` — owns the methodology: the § Durable evidence block section defines the canonical sentinel-plus-heading shape, the escalation-note rule, the body-size compact gate, and persist-time reconciliation; § Additionally persist to the durable design body defines the dual-surface persist rule and placement anchor; § 8 (Prepare the Durable Design Payload) now names the Grounding Evidence block among the material to persist, cross-referencing the canonical-shape section.
- `agents/Solution-Designer.agent.md` — owns the agent-side persist step: Stage 4's issue-update enumeration now names the block, and the Completion Gate gained the corresponding checklist item, per the thin-shell convention (shared body edits carry the actual behavior change; entry-point/version-bump concerns are separate).
- `.github/scripts/grounding-evidence-corpus-check.ps1` (with its Pester fixtures at `.github/scripts/Tests/grounding-evidence-corpus-check.Tests.ps1`) — the diagnostic script and its own test file, kept separate from the pre-existing three-file producer/persist/consumer contract test (`.github/scripts/Tests/design-grounding-discipline.Tests.ps1`, whose docstring charter was widened alongside this work to cover the delivery-route assertions but does not extend to corpus measurement).
